§ 01 — The Problem

Modern AI agents
fail at consistency.

Current agentic frameworks are built on probabilistic foundations — they chain prompts, route tools, and hope the LLM stays on track. In regulated enterprise environments, hope is not an operational primitive.

Non-deterministic execution

Same input, different outcomes. No repeatability guarantee. Impossible to audit or certify.

No long-horizon coherence

Context windows expire. Goals drift. Operational state is lost. Multi-step execution degrades silently.

Constraint blindness

Frameworks execute what's possible, not what's permissible. Regulatory constraints are afterthoughts.

Opaque decision paths

When something goes wrong, there's no trace. No reasoning chain. No accountability layer.

The missing layer

Planning is the missing layer between LLMs and reliable enterprise operations.

Orchestration frameworks give you tool routing and prompt chaining. What they don't give you is a reasoning engine that understands goals, evaluates constraints, maintains state, and produces explainable decisions — deterministically.
